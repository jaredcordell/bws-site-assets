Common questions

Merchant services questions, answered.

What does the audit cost?

Nothing. We review your statement and show you exactly what you're paying and what you could save, with zero obligation to switch.

Will switching disrupt my business?

No. If you decide to move forward, we manage the entire transition so there's no downtime at the register.

What is interchange-plus pricing?

It's the most transparent pricing model in payments: you pay the true network cost plus a small, fixed margin, so you always know what you're paying for.

Do I have to leave my current processor to get the audit?

No. The audit is informational. You only switch if the numbers clearly make sense for you.

What size business is this for?

There's no strict minimum. We work with businesses of all sizes, though the larger your processing volume, the more these savings tend to compound. If you're not sure whether it's worth a look, the audit is free either way.

How much could I realistically save?

It depends on your current setup, but most of the savings come from eliminating padded markups and junk fees (PCI fees, statement fees, batch fees) that have nothing to do with your actual processing cost. Typical clients see 25% or more off their current rate once those are removed.

What if I'm locked into a contract with my current processor?

It's worth checking anyway. Early termination penalties are usually small compared to what you'd save by switching, so even with a fee, you often come out ahead. We'll run the numbers so you can see exactly where you stand before deciding.

Will I need new equipment?

Not always. If your current hardware works with the new setup, we keep it. If a change makes sense for your business, we'll recommend equipment that actually fits how you operate, not a generic terminal.

What information do I need to provide besides my statement?

In most cases, your statement is enough to get started. If anything else is needed, we'll tell you exactly what and why, no blanket paperwork requests.
