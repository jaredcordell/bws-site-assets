Common questions

R&D credit questions, answered.

What industries typically qualify?

More than most owners expect. Manufacturing, software, engineering, construction, food and beverage, agriculture, and architecture are common — but any business solving technical problems through design, testing, or process improvement may qualify.

Does it matter if our R&D failed or the project didn't work?

No. The credit rewards the process of experimentation, not the outcome. If you faced technical uncertainty and worked through it — even if the result didn't pan out — that work can still qualify.

Can the credit be used if we're not profitable yet?

Often, yes. If your business has under $31M in gross receipts and hasn't generated revenue for more than five years, you may qualify as a small business and apply the credit against payroll tax instead of income tax — so being pre-revenue or not yet profitable doesn't automatically rule you out.

Will this raise a red flag with the IRS?

No. The R&D credit is a legitimate part of the tax code designed to reward innovation. We build a documented, four-part-test study specifically so the claim is defensible — and we stand behind it.

Will this trigger an audit?

No. Claiming the R&D credit does not increase your audit risk on its own. Our documentation is built to the four-part-test standard specifically so it holds up if the IRS ever asks questions.

We already have a CPA. Does this conflict?

Not at all. We complement your CPA. We do the specialized study and hand over a turnkey package they can file, or we coordinate directly with them.

What do you need from us to get started?

Just a short conversation to start. If you move forward, we'll ask for basic payroll and project information — we handle the heavy lifting and keep your time investment minimal.

How long does the whole process take?

Most studies are completed in a few weeks from kickoff to final documentation, depending on how quickly we can gather your project and payroll details.

How far back can we claim?

Generally up to three open tax years can be amended, which often means a refund for past work in addition to the current year.

How is the credit different from a deduction?

A deduction lowers the income you're taxed on. A credit reduces the tax you actually owe, dollar for dollar against your tax liability — making it more valuable than a deduction of the same size.

What does it cost?

Nothing upfront. Our fee is 24% of the credit secured for you. If the IRS pays interest on top of that, it's yours — we don't take a cut. No recovery, no fee.

What if you don't find anything?

Then you owe us nothing. The qualification review is free, and we only get paid from credits we help you claim — there's no downside to finding out.
