Common questions

Unclaimed property questions, answered.

What is unclaimed property?

It's money and assets owed to your business that ended up held by a government agency or court, forgotten refunds, vendor credits, uncashed checks, bankruptcy distributions, and more. When it goes unclaimed long enough, it's turned over to the state or another agency to hold.

What does it cost?

There are no up-front costs. FRS UP does the research and manages the entire recovery, so there's no risk in finding out what you're owed.

How much of my team's time does this take?

Very little. FRS UP handles the research, legal work, claim preparation, filing, and agency communication. You're only looped in to approve and to answer the occasional question.

Where does the money come from?

State unclaimed property offices, bankruptcy courts, and federal, city, and county agencies in the U.S., and, in some cases, foreign jurisdictions.

What if funds are under an old name or address?

That's common, and it's exactly why so much goes unrecovered. FRS UP advocates with the agencies to prove the funds belong to you, even when they're listed under a misspelled name, prior address, or former entity.

Does this cover companies we've acquired or sold?

Yes. Recovery is pursued for all eligible business units, subsidiaries, divisions, acquisitions, and divestitures, not just your primary company.
